About the Map - Environmental Formulas

1. Soil Quality Index (SQI)

The Soil Quality Index (SQI) is calculated based on various soil properties such as pH, organic matter, moisture content, and nutrient levels. The general formula for SQI is:

Formula:

                SQI = ((P₁×W₁) + (P₂×W₂) + ... + (Pₙ×Wₙ)) / W_total

Where:

  • Pᵢ: Value of the i-th soil property (e.g., pH, organic matter, moisture content, nutrient levels)
  • Wᵢ: Weighting factor for the i-th soil property (reflects the importance of each property in assessing soil quality)
  • n: Total number of soil properties considered
  • W_total: Sum of all weighting factors (W₁ + W₂ + ... + Wₙ)

Abbreviation: SQI stands for Soil Quality Index.

2. Pest Activity Index (PAI)

The Pest Activity Index (PAI) evaluates the pest count in a specific area over time. This formula helps in assessing how pest activity affects crop or soil health.

Formula:

                PAI = Total Pest Count / (Area × Time Factor)

Where:

  • Total Pest Count: Cumulative count of pests observed during the monitoring period
  • Area: Size of the area in square units (e.g., square feet or square meters)
  • Time Factor: A multiplier based on the length of the observation period (e.g., weekly monitoring could have a factor of 4 for a month)

Abbreviation: PAI stands for Pest Activity Index.

3. Air Quality Index (AQI)

The Air Quality Index (AQI) is calculated to assess the quality of the air in a specific region, based on pollutant concentrations.

Formula:

                I = ((C - C_low) / (C_high - C_low)) × (I_high - I_low) + I_low

Where:

  • I: AQI value for the pollutant
  • C: Pollutant concentration
  • C_low, C_high: Concentration breakpoints for lower and higher AQI categories
  • I_low, I_high: AQI values corresponding to those breakpoints

4. Temperature Suitability Formula (TI)

The Temperature Index (TI) formula calculates the suitability of a region's temperature for crop growth or similar metrics, based on optimal temperature ranges.

Formula:

                S = 1 - ((T_opt, max - T_opt, min) / (T_max - T_opt, min)) × ((A_opt - A_year) / A_opt) × ((T_opt, max - T_opt, min) / (T_avg, year - T_opt, avg))

Where:

  • S: Calculated suitability score
  • T_max: Maximum temperature for the given period
  • T_opt, min: Minimum optimal temperature for the crop
  • T_opt, max: Maximum optimal temperature for the crop
  • A_year: Annual crop-related value (yield, growth rate, etc.)
  • A_opt: Optimal annual value for the crop
  • T_avg, year: Average temperature for the year
  • T_opt, avg: Average optimal temperature for the crop

Abbreviation: TI stands for Temperature Index.
